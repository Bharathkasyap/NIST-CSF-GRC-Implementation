# Project Charter — NIST CSF 2.0 GRC Implementation

**Project Title:** Enterprise NIST CSF 2.0 Cybersecurity Program Implementation
**Organization:** TechNova Financial Services, Inc.
**Project Sponsor:** David Mercer, CISO
**Project Lead:** Venkata Bharath Kasyap Devulapalli, GRC Analyst
**Date:** January 6, 2025
**Version:** 1.0 (Approved)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Following a near-miss ransomware incident in Q3 2024 and heightened scrutiny from the OCC and FFIEC, TechNova Financial Services' Board of Directors approved a formal 12-month cybersecurity program overhaul. This project will implement the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 as the foundational governance structure, producing measurable improvements in security maturity, a comprehensive policy suite, and audit-ready compliance documentation.

---

## 2. Project Scope

### In Scope
- All corporate information systems and applications (on-premises and cloud)
- All data processing environments handling Restricted or Confidential data
- All third-party vendor relationships with system access or data handling
- Security policies, procedures, and standards across all business units
- Security Operations Center (SOC) capabilities and tooling
- Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery programs
- Security awareness training program

### Out of Scope
- Physical security controls (managed under separate Facilities Security Program)
- International subsidiaries (covered by separate regional compliance program)
- Product/application security (covered under Software Development Lifecycle program)

## 3. Business Objectives

| # | Objective | Success Measure | Target Date |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | Improve NIST CSF maturity from Tier 1 to Tier 3 | Average maturity >= 2.8/4.0 across all 6 functions | Dec 2025 |
| 2 | Reduce critical/high risk findings | Reduce from 106 to < 20 open findings | Sep 2025 |
| 3 | Achieve audit readiness for FFIEC examination | Pass mock audit with < 5 major findings | Nov 2025 |
| 4 | Establish continuous monitoring program | Program operational with documented BAU procedures | Dec 2025 |
| 5 | Build security governance foundation | 14 policies approved and published | Jun 2025 |
| 6 | Reduce phishing susceptibility | Click rate < 10% in quarterly simulations | Sep 2025 |

## 7. Assumptions and Constraints

**Assumptions:**
- Executive leadership will provide timely responses to information requests (SLA: 5 business days)
- Existing tooling (Microsoft Sentinel, Qualys) will be available and licensed throughout the project
- Department heads will designate a single point of contact for each business unit
- No major organizational restructuring will occur during project execution

**Constraints:**
- FFIEC examination scheduled for Q4 2025 — all evidence must be ready by October 2025
- Budget is fixed at $1.4M for Year 1 — scope changes require CISO approval
- IT change freeze periods (Q4 earnings, holiday season) limit implementation windows

---

## 8. Risks to the Project

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Key stakeholder unavailability delaying reviews | Medium | High | Buffer time built into each phase; escalation path defined |
| Tool procurement delays (CyberArk) | Low | High | Identified backup vendor; procurement started in Phase 0 |
| Scope creep from new regulatory requirements | Medium | Medium | Change control process; CISO approval required |
| Staff turnover (key IT Security personnel) | Low | High | Knowledge transfer sessions; documentation-first approach |
| Ransomware incident during project | Low | Critical | Emergency response plan; project continuity documented |
